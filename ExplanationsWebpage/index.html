Fault v. Contingency

Faults you do not anticipate to ever happen ---unless there’s a bug in your code. Contingencies are error conditions you expect to happen rarely, but as part of the production logic. An example of a contingency is if a user of a bank app tries to overdraw their checking account. Contingencies should be handled in a robust manner. For faults, there’s often nothing better to do than carefully log the error occurred and prevent the larger application from crashing, where possible.
